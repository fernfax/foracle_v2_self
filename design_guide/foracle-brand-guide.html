Brand Foundations
Tagline
your financial home, in Singapore
Personal, grounded, and specific. Not a generic fintech promise — a place that belongs to you. The lowercase and comma pause are intentional: they make it feel human, not corporate. Always in Lora italic. Never modified.
Mission — what we do
To give every Singaporean a complete, clear view of their financial life — and the confidence to act on it.
We help people understand and improve their financial habits over time.
Vision — where we're going
Financial clarity for everyone.
A world where people feel in control of their money — not afraid of it.
Brand positioning — value proposition
"A modern, minimal, and approachable money companion that helps Singaporeans stay in control — without stress or judgment."
We want something clean, modern, and useful — but still human and approachable. A no-nonsense money tool that feels simple and friendly, not stressful or intimidating.
Brand character — the guided companion
Foracle is best understood as a Guided Companion — a financial co-pilot that works quietly in the background, surfacing just-in-time insights that reveal themselves over time. The name itself signals this: Oracle = quiet intelligence. Not loud, not pushy, not preachy. Just there when you need it, with the right information at the right moment.

Design philosophy
Foracle's visual identity sits at the intersection of two qualities: the warmth of Singapore's Nanyang heritage and the precision of a modern, minimal product. The brand resolves this tension by assigning each quality to a distinct system — warmth lives in the colour palette, typography choices, and voice; precision lives in the layout grid, spacing system, and data presentation.
The soul — Nanyang warmth
Earthy terracotta primary · Kaya gold · Jungle green · Warm cream backgrounds · Lora serif for emotional moments · Peranakan tile motif signature
The discipline — minimal SG
Space Grotesk for all UI · Soft, approachable shapes · Clean minimal layouts · Teal reserved for positive states only · Precise 8px grid

Colour System
Philosophy — light-first, dark-supported
Light mode is Foracle's primary, default experience. It reflects the brand's warmth and approachability — open, calm, and friendly. Dark mode is a supported option for users who prefer it during evening use or extended financial review sessions. Both modes share the same accent colours and semantic rules; only the surface and text tokens change.
Terracotta family
The standout accent. CTAs, active states, logo. Shifts one step lighter in dark mode for contrast.
Teal Flash — locked
Identical hex in both modes. Its meaning (positive progress) must never feel different between modes.
Kaya Gold — locked
Identical hex in both modes. Warning and attention states must feel consistent regardless of mode.

Colours locked across both modes
These three accent colours carry semantic meaning that must feel identical whether a user is in light or dark mode. Using different shades would break the emotional signal system.
Teal Flash — #00C4AA
Positive financial progress. On track, under budget, goal reached, savings milestone. Never used for actions or navigation. Same in both modes — its meaning must never feel different.
Kaya Gold — #D4A843
Attention and warning. Renewals due, 80%+ of budget, review needed, policy expiring. Same in both modes — the "pay attention" signal must be consistent.
Alert Red — shifted slightly
#E05555 in light mode, #E07070 in dark. The same family, slightly lightened for legibility on dark surfaces. This is the only semantic colour that adjusts — and only for contrast, never for meaning.
Semantic colour rules — the teal rule
Teal (#00C4AA) has one job: signal positive financial progress. It must never appear on primary buttons, navigation, or neutral UI elements. This deliberate scarcity gives it weight — when a user sees teal, they know something good has happened.

Typography
Type system — two fonts, two roles
Foracle uses exactly two typefaces. Space Grotesk handles all functional, data-facing, and UI type. Lora italic is reserved exclusively for emotional, editorial, and brand moments. This creates a clear rule: if it's about doing, use Space Grotesk; if it's about feeling, use Lora.
foracle
Wordmark · Space Grotesk 600
Always lowercase. Letter-spacing: -1.5px. The 'acle' suffix carries the terracotta accent colour.
Your money, beautifully kept.
Display H1 · Space Grotesk 600
32–40px. Marketing headlines, screen titles, onboarding headers. Letter-spacing: -0.5px to -1px.
Your complete financial picture.
Heading H2 · Space Grotesk 600
20–24px. Section headers, feature titles, card headings.
your financial home, in Singapore.
Editorial · Lora Italic
Reserved only for: taglines, marketing hero copy, brand moments, and the positioning statement. Never use for UI copy or data labels.
Track expenses, manage goals, and plan for your family — all in one place, built for how Singaporeans really live and plan their financial futures.
Body · DM Sans 400
14–16px. All body copy, descriptions, onboarding text, notifications. Line-height: 1.6–1.75.
Monthly expenses · November 2025
Label Caps · Space Grotesk 500
10–11px. All data labels, chart axes, metadata, section sub-labels. Always uppercase. Letter-spacing: 0.14–0.18em.
S$41,200
Data Display · Space Grotesk 600
Currency and key numbers. Always Space Grotesk, never DM Sans. Size scales with importance: 24–36px.
Typography rules
Do
Use Space Grotesk for all button labels, navigation items, data labels, and UI copy.

Use Lora italic sparingly — maximum one instance per screen or page.

Maintain hierarchy: Display > H2 > Body > Label. Never skip levels.

Use negative letter-spacing on large display type (-0.5px to -1.5px at 24px+).

All wordmark usage must be lowercase with no exceptions.
Don't
Never use Lora upright (non-italic) — it only appears in italic form.

Never mix DM Sans and Space Grotesk in the same UI element (e.g. a button).

Never use all-caps for Display or H2 type — only Label Caps are capped.

Never use font sizes below 11px anywhere in the product.

Never use Lora for data, numbers, labels, or functional copy.

05
The Tile Motif
What it is
The tile motif is Foracle's brand signature element. It is a horizontal strip of six equal-width colour blocks — one per brand colour — tiled and repeating. The reference is the decorative border band that runs along Peranakan shophouse facades and edges of nyonya ceramics. Here it is completely abstracted into pure geometry: no florals, no curves, just the colour rhythm. This is the hybrid in a single element — Nanyang soul, modern execution.
The base sequence — always in this order
One fixed rule: The colour sequence never changes — Deep Forest → Terracotta → Teal Flash → Jungle Green → Kaya Gold → Prawn Coral → repeat. This consistency is what makes it a recognisable system.

08
Voice & Tone
Brand voice — the four qualities
Warm
We speak like a trusted friend — someone who gets it, not a financial institution reading from a script.
Not: overly casual or slangy
Direct
We say what we mean. No hedging, no jargon, no fine print buried in the copy.
Not: blunt or dismissive
Local
We speak Singaporean English when it fits. BTO, CPF, hawker — we don't translate what our users already know.
Not: parochial or exclusive
Encouraging
Money is stressful. We reduce that stress with clarity, not by avoiding hard truths.
Not: cheerleader-y or patronising
Voice in practice
Do write this
"You've spent 68% of your November budget. You're on track — just watch the dining category."
Clear, specific, helpful. Doesn't alarm unnecessarily.
Don't write this
"Warning: 68.3% budget utilisation detected. Projected overspend risk identified in F&B expenditure category."
Clinical, corporate, anxiety-inducing. Sounds like a bank statement.
Do write this
"Your BTO goal is 69% there. At this rate, you'll hit your target by March 2026."
Grounds hope in data. Specific and motivating.
Don't write this
"Congratulations!!! You're SO close to your savings dream! Keep going, superstar! 🎉🎉🎉"
Gimmicky, patronising. Undermines the seriousness of the financial goal.
Do write this
"Your NTUC Income policy renews in 23 days. Want us to remind you to review your coverage?"
SG-native (NTUC Income), proactive, respects user's time.
Don't write this
"ACTION REQUIRED: Insurance policy expiry imminent. Failure to renew may result in coverage lapse."
Fear-based, formal, sounds like a government notice.
SG-specific language
Use freely
  • BTO, HDB, CPF, OA, SA, MA, RA
  • SRS, CPFIS, MediShield Life
  • S$ (not SGD in UI copy)
  • Hawker, kopitiam, MRT
  • Kiasu (used warmly, not as stereotype)
Use carefully
  • Singlish particles (lah, lor) — sparingly, never in error messages
  • Racial or ethnic cultural references
  • Political references
  • Avoid "we Singaporeans" — can feel exclusionary to PRs and expats
