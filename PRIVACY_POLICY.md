## 2. The personal data we collect

Foracle is a personal finance tool, so much of the data you enter is sensitive
financial information. We collect:

### 2.1 Account and identity data
- Name and email address (via our authentication provider, Clerk)
- Profile image (if you provide one)
- Account and onboarding status

### 2.2 Financial data you enter
- **Income** — sources, amounts, employment details, CPF contributions
  (employee/employer, Ordinary/Special/MediSave allocations), bonuses, take-home
  pay, and income history
- **Expenses** — categories, amounts, frequencies, daily spending, notes, and
  budget allocations
- **Assets and liabilities** — property (including purchase price, loans,
  CPF withdrawn, housing grants, accrued interest), vehicles (including COE
  details and loans), investments, bank/cash holdings and their history
- **Insurance policies** — provider, plan, policy number, policy type, premiums
  (cash and CPF), coverage, beneficiaries, cash values, and maturity dates
- **Goals** — financial targets, amounts, and timelines

### 2.3 Household / family member data
- Names, relationship, date of birth, and contribution status of household or
  family members you add, and any income, expenses, or holdings you associate
  with them. **You provide this on the basis that you are authorised to do so**
  (see Section 6).

### 2.4 AI assistant interactions
- The messages you send to the AI assistant and the account data drawn upon to
  answer them.

### 2.5 Documents you upload
- Any files or documents you upload, and text/embeddings derived from them for
  search and assistant features.

### 2.6 Technical and usage data
- Authentication tokens and session data, device/browser information, IP
  address, log data, and basic usage information needed to operate and secure
  the Service.

We do **not** connect to your bank accounts or pull data from CPF, IRAS, or your
insurers automatically — you enter and control what Foracle sees.

## 3. How we use your personal data (purposes)

We use personal data only for purposes for which you have given (or are deemed
under the PDPA to have given) consent, including to:

1. create and manage your account and authenticate you;
2. provide the Service — store, display, calculate, project, and visualise your
   financial information;
3. operate the AI assistant and document-search features;
4. maintain, secure, troubleshoot, and improve the Service;
5. respond to your enquiries and provide support;
6. send you service-related communications (e.g. security or material changes);
7. comply with legal and regulatory obligations and enforce our Terms; and
8. detect, prevent, and address fraud, abuse, or security incidents.

We do **not** use your financial data to make automated decisions that produce
legal or similarly significant effects on you, and we do **not** sell your
personal data.

| Recipient | Purpose | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Clerk** | Authentication and account management | Holds name, email, login credentials |

We may also disclose personal data where required by law, court order, or a
lawful request by a public authority, or to protect our rights, users, or the

## 8. Data security (Protection Obligation)

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to
protect personal data against unauthorised access, use, disclosure, loss, or
alteration. These include access controls and per-user data isolation,
encryption in transit, authentication via a dedicated provider, and restricting
staff access on a need-to-know basis. No system is completely secure, however,
and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## 9. Your rights (Access, Correction, Withdrawal)

Under the PDPA you may:

- **Access** — request information about the personal data we hold about you and
  how it has been used or disclosed in the past year;
- **Correct** — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data;
- **Withdraw consent** — withdraw your consent to our collection, use, or
  disclosure of your personal data, with reasonable notice; and
- **Delete your account** — close your account and request deletion of your data,
  subject to Section 11 and our Data Retention Policy.

To exercise any of these rights, contact our DPO at `[DPO EMAIL]`. We will
respond within the timeframe required by the PDPA (generally within 30 days, or

an access request and may need to verify your identity first.

## 10. Data breach notification

We maintain procedures to detect, assess, and respond to data breaches. In line
with the PDPA's **Data Breach Notification Obligation**, if a breach is assessed
to be notifiable — that is, it is likely to result in significant harm to
affected individuals, or it affects **500 or more individuals** — we will notify
the **PDPC within 3 calendar days** of determining that the breach is notifiable,
and we will notify **affected individuals as soon as practicable** where the
breach is likely to result in significant harm to them.

## 11. How long we keep your data (retention)

We keep personal data only for as long as necessary to provide the Service and
to meet legal, accounting, or reporting obligations, in line with the PDPA's
**Retention Limitation Obligation** and our [Data Retention Policy](./DATA_RETENTION_POLICY.md).
When personal data is no longer needed, we will delete it or anonymise it. In
summary:

- **Active accounts** — kept while your account is active.
- **Account deletion** — your financial and account data is deleted or anonymised
  within the period set out in our Data Retention Policy after you delete your
  account, except where we must retain certain records to comply with the law or
  to resolve disputes.
- **Backups and logs** — may persist for a limited additional period before being
  overwritten or purged.
